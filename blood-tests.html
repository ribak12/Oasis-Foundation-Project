Ultrasound Services

Clear, guided imaging for pregnancy follow-ups, abdominal concerns, pelvic scans, and routine diagnostic evaluation in a calm clinical setting.

Our ultrasound service is designed to make diagnostic imaging feel simpler and less stressful. Patients can quickly understand the scan options, know how to prepare, and feel more confident about what to expect before arriving.

Typical slot15 to 30 minMost routine scans are completed comfortably within a short visit.
ReportsSame-day for many scansRoutine reports are usually shared quickly after review.
Best forPregnancy and diagnostic imagingHelpful for obstetric, abdominal, pelvic, and Doppler needs.
Scans Available
  • Pregnancy and obstetric scans including growth and wellbeing follow-up
  • Abdominal ultrasound for liver, gall bladder, kidney, and digestive concerns
  • Pelvic scans for gynecological and lower abdominal evaluation
  • Thyroid and neck soft-tissue assessment
  • Doppler studies when blood flow assessment is advised
  • Follicular monitoring and cycle tracking support
Before You Come
  • A full bladder may be needed for selected pelvic scans
  • Fasting may be advised for certain abdominal scans
  • Carry old reports, prescriptions, or referral notes if available
  • Wear comfortable clothing for an easier examination
  • Arriving a little early helps with registration and preparation
Good to Visit For
  • Routine pregnancy monitoring and reassurance scans
  • Abdominal pain, swelling, bloating, or suspected stone-related issues
  • Pelvic pain, menstrual concerns, or follow-up advised by your doctor
  • Requested imaging before treatment or specialist consultation
Why Patients Choose This Service

Patients choose this service because the process is straightforward, the scan types are clearly guided, and the preparation is explained in advance. It is built to feel efficient, supportive, and easy to understand from start to finish.

Radiation-free imaging Guided support Easy report follow-up
Frequently Asked
Do I need to fast?Only for some abdominal scans. If your doctor or staff advised fasting, plain water is usually fine unless told otherwise.
How long does the scan take?Many scans are completed within 15 to 30 minutes, though the exact time depends on the scan type.
Should I bring old reports?Yes. Previous scans, prescriptions, and test reports can help with comparison and better clinical context.

Oxygen Rental Services

Home-care oxygen support with clearer details on available equipment, setup help, prescription requirements, and safe-use guidance.

Our oxygen rental service is planned for families who need reliable home-care support with less confusion. The information below explains equipment options, required documents, and the safety steps that matter before arranging a rental.

OptionsCylinders and concentratorsSuitable for short-term need as well as longer home-care support.
SupportDelivery and setup helpAssistance includes installation guidance and usage guidance.
RequirementPrescription neededMedical oxygen is provided only with proper doctor advice and documentation.
Available Equipment
  • B-type portable cylinder for shorter mobility-focused needs
  • D-type cylinder for longer backup duration
  • 5 LPM and 10 LPM oxygen concentrator options
  • Required accessories such as regulator, flowmeter, tubing, and humidifier support
Rental Support Included
  • Delivery based on location and availability
  • Basic setup assistance at handover
  • Refill coordination for cylinder-based rentals
  • Help for troubleshooting and use-related questions
Documents and Safety
  • Doctor prescription is mandatory before issue
  • ID proof and basic rental details may be required
  • Keep equipment upright and away from open flame or smoke
  • Use only the oxygen flow advised by the doctor
Why Families Choose This Service

Families choose this service because it clearly explains the available equipment, provides setup support, and makes the rental process easier to understand during urgent or recovery-related situations.

Home-care ready Setup guidance Availability by call
Frequently Asked
What is the difference between a cylinder and a concentrator?A cylinder stores oxygen for use until it empties, while a concentrator generates oxygen continuously from room air when powered.
Is setup support provided?Yes. Basic usage guidance and setup support are part of the rental experience so the family can use the equipment more confidently.
Can I get oxygen without a prescription?No. A prescription is required for safety and proper medical use.
